# Networking templates for review

These are deliberately structured drafts, not finished generic copy. Each daily target must
replace every bracketed field with observed context. The generator refuses blank signal,
relevance, topic, or proof fields, and it never sends messages.

## Required personalization brief

- Contact: `[full name]`, `[exact role]`, `[company]`, `[LinkedIn URL]`
- Company signal: `[specific launch, article, talk, hiring initiative, or transformation]`
- Relevance: `[why that signal connects to Evan's current work or target role]`
- Proof: `[one X-Y-Z achievement: result, metric/scale, and method]`
- Discussion topic: `[one narrow question this person is unusually qualified to answer]`
- Shared context: `[optional genuine overlap—do not manufacture one]`
- Target role: `[optional exact vacancy title]`

## LinkedIn connection note

> Hi [First name] — your work as [role] at [company] stood out, especially [company signal].
> [One-sentence relevance] I’d value connecting.

The generator caps this at 300 characters.

## LinkedIn follow-up

> Hi [First name], thanks for connecting. [Genuine shared context, if any]
>
> I reached out because [company signal]. [Relevance] My relevant experience: [X-Y-Z proof].
>
> I’m curious about [narrow topic] [while exploring exact role, if applicable]. If it would be
> useful, I’d be happy to share what I’m seeing from the practitioner side as well.
>
> Evan

## Email request

> Subject: A focused question about [narrow topic]
>
> Hi [First name],
>
> I am reaching out because [company signal]. [Relevance]
>
> My closest relevant experience is [X-Y-Z proof]. I would value your perspective on [narrow
> topic]. Would you be open to a focused 20-minute call in the next two weeks? I will send
> three questions beforehand and work around your schedule.
>
> Best,
> Evan

## Coffee-chat request

> Hi [First name],
>
> I’ve been following [company]’s work on [company signal]. Your perspective as [role] is
> particularly relevant because [relevance].
>
> I’ve worked on [X-Y-Z proof], and I’m trying to understand [narrow topic] from someone close
> to the work—not ask for a referral. Would you be open to a focused 20-minute coffee chat in
> the next two weeks? I’ll send three questions in advance and work around your schedule.
>
> Best,
> Evan

## Daily review checklist

- Verify the company signal is current and attributable to this contact or their team.
- Replace broad praise with one concrete observation.
- Use one proof point relevant to this person; do not paste a resume summary.
- Keep the question narrow enough to discuss in 20 minutes.
- Remove the target-job reference when the goal is relationship-building rather than recruiting.
- Send only after manually reviewing tone, facts, timing, and any prior conversation history.

## Coffee-chat preparation packet

The daily artifact also generates:

- a one-sentence learning objective that explicitly avoids asking for a referral;
- a 30-second opening grounded in the selected proof point;
- five contact- and topic-specific questions;
- a value-to-offer reminder so the conversation is reciprocal without becoming a pitch;
- a close that asks permission to keep in touch;
- a follow-up draft with placeholders for the actual insight and promised resource;
- structured notes for insight, implication, resource/person mentioned, promise, and follow-up date.
